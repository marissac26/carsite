Iron & Steele

Traditional Hot Rods and Custom Roadsters

Iron and Steele is the brainchild of Jake Steele a car builder, author and automotive historian from Portland, Oregon. Jake is a staple in the hot rod community as a frequent feature writer for Hop Up, Rod and Kulture and in his spare time co-founded The Estranged Car Club. Seeing the need for a web-based community highlighting traditional hot rods, the history of the phenomenon and the people involved, Iron and Steele was born. It is a collaboration of individuals passionate about the greatest hobby on earth: Hot rods. It is meant to cater to not only the die-hard car guys, but also to invite people from all walks of life to see what all the fuss is about through amazing photography and creative story telling

1931 Model A:

  • A period-perfect representation of a true 1930's hot rod with a 5" chop
  • 3" dropped axle in the front, split wish bones
  • model a block, bored .040 over
  • single Stromberg 97 downdraft carburetor and pressurized fuel system
  • custom made exhaust manifold

1936 Ford Coupe:

  • A traditionally styled, completed to accurately reflect a late 1950's era custom
  • lowered 8" in the rear, 4" in the front
  • one piece molded rear fenders
  • shaved cowl, door lock cylinders & filled roof
  • early small block Chevrolet powered
  • three Stromberg carburetors

1940 Willys:

  • Built to emulate a 1960's drag race "gasser"
  • chevrolet 350CI engine, bored .060 over, ported, polished and relieved block
  • Muncie M21 close-ratio 4 spd manual trans
  • ford 9" rear end, spooled
  • bed shortened 12", lightened
  • custom exhaust feat removable header caps

This is a story that has a lot to do with new beginnings, life altering occurrences, chance encounters and life long friendships. In very brief terms, it is the epitome of the spirit of Iron and Steele. It was the spring of 2008 and I was living a decent suburban existence. It was your typical scene: 9 to 5 job, 5 to 9 wife, a house and a lot of dreaming about hot rods. It was the kind of thing you would mostly expect from a 26 year old guy with a lot of ideas that were mostly bigger than his means, but I was getting by just fine and even making progress on a Model A coupe that I had bought as just a body a few months before from a local guy I had met through the Jalopy Journal. When I say “progress”, I mean that I had mostly acquired parts I thought I would need to make the project a reality and had done a really great job of several “mock ups”.
